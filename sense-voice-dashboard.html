Sense AI Recruiter - Top of Funnel Analysis

Date Range analyzed • [2025-04-01 → 2026-02-01]

Executive Summary

This analysis reviews Sense AI Recruiter's Top-of-Funnel (TOF) performance over a 10 month period from April 2025 upto February 2026, covering 20,284 calls to 11,172 unique candidates.

The platform delivered a 77.7% connection rate (8,684 connected calls), reflecting strong initial engagement at scale. Engagement quality remains solid, with 55.5% of connected candidates progressing through to interview completion. Overall, this results in an 43.2% end-to-end conversion rate (4,821 completed interviews), demonstrating consistent funnel performance and effective downstream execution.

Call timing significantly impacts performance: evening windows (4PM-10PM show 48-53% connection rates) substantially outperform morning and midday slots (24-39% connection rates), indicating opportunity for strategic outreach scheduling.

Primary connection challenges stem from voicemail (4,908 calls) and no-answer scenarios (3,751 calls), while in-call drops are driven by candidate hang-ups (2,127) and agent-initiated disconnects (1,380).

Critical Findings

Strong Connection Performance
Finding: 77.7% connection rate demonstrates effective candidate reach, though 22.3% never connected despite averaging 1.82 attempts per candidate
Evening Windows Significantly Outperform
Finding: 4PM-10PM slots achieve 48-53% connection rates vs 24-39% during business hours, implying strategic timing could materially improve reach efficiency
Healthy Post-Connection Completion
Finding: 55.5% of connected candidates complete interviews which indicates strong interview quality and candidate experience once engaged
Voicemail Strategy Gap
Finding: 4,908 calls (42.3% of no-contact) reach voicemail. Focusing on optimizing voicemail messaging or callback triggers could recover significant volume
Candidate-Initiated Drops Dominate
Finding: 55.1% of in-call drops are candidate hang-ups, which could suggest early conversation friction or mis-targeting requiring script/qualification review
Positive Sentiment Signal
Finding: 29.0% positive sentiment (5,888 calls) vs 1.2% negative (241) indicates strong candidate experience indicator despite drop challenges
