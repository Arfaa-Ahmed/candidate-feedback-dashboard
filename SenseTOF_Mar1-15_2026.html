Sense AI Recruiter – Top of Funnel Analysis

Date Range analyzed • [2026-03-01 → 2026-03-15]

Executive Summary

This analysis reviews Sense AI Recruiter's Top-of-Funnel (TOF) performance over a 15-day period from March 1 to March 15, 2026, across 4,685 call attempts representing 1,865 unique candidates., 67.9% of candidates successfully connected (1,267 unique) with the AI recruiter and 45.1% ultimately completed the interaction (842 unique).

Once a candidate connects, completion performance remains strong at 66.5%, indicating that in-call experience quality is generally healthy. The platform averages 2.51 attempts per candidate, with positive sentiment recorded on 60.5% of connected calls.

Primary connection challenges stem from voicemail (1,158 calls) and blank/missing disconnection reasons (1,069 calls), while in-call drops are driven by candidate hang-ups (524) and agent-initiated disconnects (231).

Critical Findings

Strong Connection & Conversion Profile
Finding: 67.9% connection rate and 45.1% end-to-end conversion are healthy top-of-funnel metrics for a 15-day window. Once connected, 66.5% complete, indicating the voice flow itself is performing well. Positive sentiment at 60.5% of connected calls reinforces that candidates are engaging substantively.
Afternoon Peak - Strategic Scheduling Opportunity
Finding: Local hours 2PM–3PM achieve 46–47% connection rates, with the broader 10AM–6PM window sustaining 40%+ rates. Early morning (3AM–5AM) drops to 10–18%. Shifting more volume into the 2PM–4PM sweet spot could improve yield without increasing total attempts.
Early Candidate Drop-off Signal
Finding: 64.8% of in-call drops (524 calls, 406 unique candidates) are user hang-ups, with a median terminated call duration of 45 seconds. Candidates are disengaging very early, likely within the opening script or initial qualification. Reviewing the first 60 seconds of the voice flow is a high-priority optimization.
Blank Disconnection Reasons - Data Quality Drag
Finding: 1,069 no-contact calls (35.4% of drops, affecting 1,008 unique candidates) have no disconnection reason recorded. This limits the ability to distinguish between dialer failures, carrier issues, and candidate-side problems. Investigating these calls would improve root-cause precision.
