Key Insights

Sense Strengths

  • Higher end-to-end conversion: 45.1% vs Alex's 35.6%. Voice outreach drives a 9.5pp advantage in overall yield
  • Superior connection rate: 67.9% vs Alex's 55.7%. Real-time phone engagement outperforms passive SMS activation by 12.2pp
  • Stronger post-engagement completion: 66.5% vs 63.9%. Once connected, voice interactions convert at a slightly higher rate
  • Positive sentiment signal: 60.5% positive sentiment on connected calls with only 2.4% negative, indicating strong candidate experience

Alex Strengths

  • Higher absolute completions: 1,009 vs 842 completed interviews. SMS volume advantage produces 20% more total completions
  • Quantifiable ROI: 439 recruiter hours saved in 15 days (~29 hrs/day equivalent), with clear automation leverage
  • Higher volume throughput: 2,831 invitations vs 1,865 candidates. SMS scales more easily for mass outreach
  • Strong candidate satisfaction: Ratings skew heavily toward 4–5 stars with minimal detractors

Sense Challenges

  • Lower total volume: 842 completions vs 1,009. voice is inherently less scalable than SMS
  • In-call friction: 33.5% of connected candidates don't complete. User hang-ups (524) dominate with 45s median drop time
  • Data quality gaps: 35.4% of no-contact drops have blank disconnection reasons, limiting root-cause diagnosis
  • Dedup complexity: 22.3% of rows missing phone numbers required reconciliation, inflating naive candidate counts by ~1,000

Alex Challenges

  • Activation gap: 44.3% of invitations (1,253) remain in "Sent" status, nearly half of outreach goes unactioned
  • Large paused pool: 478 candidates (16.9%) stalled in "Paused", potential recoverable volume sitting idle
  • Channel limitation: SMS requires candidate-initiated action, no mechanism for immediate real-time engagement
  • Recruiter variance: Conversion ranges from 34.3% to 51% across recruiters, assignment strategy materially affects outcomes

Critical Context

These systems are NOT directly comparable. Sense uses proactive voice calls (phone rings, candidate answers) while Alex uses passive SMS invitations (candidate must initiate scheduling). Sense's higher conversion rate (45.1% vs 35.6%) reflects the inherent advantage of real-time synchronous engagement vs asynchronous text-based activation. Direct comparison requires channel normalization.


Notably, in this 15-day window Alex delivered more total completed interviews (1,009 vs 842) despite the lower conversion rate, due to higher outreach volume. This suggests the two platforms serve complementary roles: Sense excels at high-conversion targeted outreach while Alex provides scalable, automation-driven volume with measurable recruiter time savings. The optimal strategy likely involves using both in tandem - Sense for high-priority candidates where conversion matters most, and Alex for broader pool coverage where volume and efficiency are the priority.
