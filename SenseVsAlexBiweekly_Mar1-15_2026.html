Sense vs Alex - Performance Comparison

March 1–15, 2026 • 15-Day Analysis Window

Sense AI • Voice Outreach Alex AI • SMS Interviews

Channel context: Sense uses proactive voice calls (phone rings, candidate answers) while Alex uses passive SMS invitations (candidate must initiate). These are fundamentally different engagement models - Sense's "connection" = live conversation; Alex's "response" = candidate took action on a text. This report covers both Top-of-Funnel (screening) and Bottom-of-Funnel (Salesforce pipeline outcomes) to give a complete picture of where each platform delivers value.

Matching Engine - Volume vs Quality

Sense sends up to 50 matches per job; Alex caps at 25. More volume doesn't always mean better outcomes. Chart uses log scale to make all bars comparable.

Key signal: Sense's matching engine generated 287 recruiter declines vs Alex's 36, an 8× difference. The higher interest rate on Sense (4.9% vs 1.4%) is largely a function of volume, not precision. This creates recruiter overhead and candidate fatigue from irrelevant outreach. Alex's tighter match limits keep the pipeline lean and purposeful.

Key Insights

Where Sense Leads

  • Higher conversion rates: 45.1% end-to-end vs 35.6%. Voice outreach commands immediate attention and produces a 9.5pp advantage
  • Superior connection rate: 67.9% vs 55.7%. Real-time phone engagement outperforms passive SMS by 12.2pp
  • Matching volume: 3,808 matches vs 1,110. Higher outreach ceiling produces more interested candidates (185 vs 14)

Where Alex Leads

  • Placements: 5 placements vs 0 - the metric that directly drives revenue
  • Pipeline throughput: 973 reviewed, 66 handoffs, 61 client submissions - all materially higher than Sense
  • Automation ROI: 439 hours saved in 15 days (~29 hrs/day equivalent), quantifiable recruiter capacity gain
  • Match quality: 36 recruiter declines vs 287. Less wasted recruiter effort, less candidate fatigue

Sense - Key Risks

  • Zero placements this period despite 2 offers accepted, downstream conversion is not materializing
  • In-call friction: 33.5% of connected candidates hang up, mostly within 60 seconds of the voice flow opening
  • Match quality signal: 287 recruiter declines = high "filler" volume creating overhead without proportional outcomes
  • Higher idle time: 16.4 avg days vs 14.9. Recruiter follow-through is slower on Sense-screened candidates

Alex - Key Risks

  • SMS activation gap: 44.3% (1,253) of invitations never get a response, the single largest funnel leak
  • Large paused pool: 478 candidates (16.9%) stalled, representing potentially recoverable volume
  • Lower conversion rate: 35.6% vs 45.1%. The SMS channel inherently produces lower per-candidate yield
  • Recruiter variance: Conversion ranges 34–51% across recruiters, inconsistent assignment strategy

Bottom Line

Alex is outperforming Sense on the metrics that drive revenue this period: 5 placements vs 0, 61 client submissions vs 45, 66 handoffs vs 45, and 439 hours of quantifiable automation savings. Sense holds a meaningful edge in raw conversion rates (45.1% vs 35.6%) due to the voice channel's immediacy, but that advantage is not translating into proportionally better downstream outcomes.

The matching engine comparison is particularly stark: Sense pushes 3.4× more volume but generates 8× more recruiter declines (287 vs 36) without proportionally more placements. This creates recruiter overhead and candidate fatigue without delivering the conversion that justifies the volume.
